Introduction

What is Solid Waste?

Solid material, non-liquid, and non-gaseous waste generated from domestic, commercial, industrial, and agricultural activities that has been discarded.

It is an inevitable byproduct of human existence, heavily influenced by our economic growth and technological advancement.

"There is no such thing as 'away'. When we throw anything away, it must go somewhere." — Annie Leonard

Technologies

Core Processing Methods

🌿

Composting

Biological decomposition of organic waste by microorganisms in aerated environments. Creates rich fertilizer.

♻️

Recycling

Material recovery and reprocessing of separated dry waste (plastic, paper, glass) into brand new consumer products.

🔥

Incineration (WtE)

Thermal treatment at extremely high temperatures (850-1100°C) equipped to capture energy and reduce waste volume by 90%.

💨

Biomethanation

Anaerobic digestion of wet organic waste to produce methane/biogas as fuel, leaving behind a digestate liquid fertilizer.

🏔️

Step 6

Sanitary Landfills

The final destination for reject, non-recyclable, and incombustible inert waste. Open dumping is extremely harmful, requiring engineered solutions.

Engineered Safeguards

🛡️ Bottom Liner: Thick plastic clay layers preventing soil contamination.
💧 Leachate Collection: Drainage pipes to collect toxic liquid run-offs.
💨 Methane Traps: Vents to capture greenhouse gases emitted from decay.
盖 Daily Cover: Capping waste with soil to prevent pests and odors.
